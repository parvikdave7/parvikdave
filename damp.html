Department Academic Mentorship Program (DAMP)

DAMP is a student-led initiative that helps student transition from their freshman year to sophomore year. As the curriculum goes from being generic to more department-specific, students face several issues pertaining to academics, research interests, and their future.
Through DAMP, we ensure that every student feels seen and heard, and they have a motivated and well rounded senior from their own department, someone they can build a personal connect with. We help bridge the gap between the student, department, and the faculty, and obtain feedback from the students for constant improvement of the engineering program. To visit our EE DAMP website, click here.

During the year 2021-22, I was a DAMP mentor. I was responsible for looking after 8 sophomores. It was an enriching experience mentoring students from diverse backgrounds.

In the following year, I was heading the DAMP program as one of the DAMP Coordinators (DAMPC). As a DAMPC, I was responsible for the meticulous selection of the team of mentors for the year 2022-23.
